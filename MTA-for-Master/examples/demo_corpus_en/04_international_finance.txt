International finance relies on capital flows that cross borders at high speed. Stock markets, sovereign bonds and derivative products make up most of contemporary financial exchanges. The two thousand and eight financial crisis revealed the fragilities of the global banking system and the failures of regulatory mechanisms. Investment banks, rating agencies and hedge funds were singled out for their role in spreading the crisis. Since then, prudential regulation policies have been strengthened, notably through the Basel accords which impose capital ratios on banks. Questions of tax evasion, tax havens and the taxation of financial transactions remain at the heart of economic and political debates. The public debt of states and the monetary policies of central banks largely structure global economic growth.
