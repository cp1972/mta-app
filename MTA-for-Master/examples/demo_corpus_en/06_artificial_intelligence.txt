Generative artificial intelligence raises new ethical and legal challenges for contemporary societies. Language models trained on immense corpora drawn from the web produce plausible texts, sometimes indistinguishable from human productions. Questions of intellectual property, the truthfulness of generated information and the responsibility of designers become central in public debate. Public institutions, researchers and regulators are working to develop normative frameworks adapted to these rapidly evolving technologies. The recommendation algorithms of digital platforms also shape access to information and contribute to the formation of political opinions. The automated moderation of content, the detection of false information and the transparency of algorithmic systems constitute major democratic issues. The sociology of digital technology studies these phenomena by combining the analysis of technical devices with that of the social uses that take hold of them.
