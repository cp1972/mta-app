Machine learning algorithms transform many sectors of human activity. Deep neural networks, trained on very large quantities of data, now make image recognition, automatic translation and text generation possible. Natural language processing has seen considerable progress with the arrival of transformer models and their multilingual variants. Topic modelling methods, such as non-negative matrix factorization and latent Dirichlet allocation, nevertheless remain valuable tools to explore large corpora of texts and bring out their main themes. These statistical methods automatically classify documents and identify the most representative words of each theme. The social sciences are gradually adopting these computational tools to analyze media discourse, historical archives and corpora produced by digital social networks.
